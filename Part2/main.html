Overview

Badminton is a competitive game involving 2 to 4 players each equipped with their own racket, a net located in the center of a court and a single shuttlecock.The aim of the game is to hit the shuttlecock with the racket over the net in an attempt to score points if it hits an opponent's side of the court.

Rules

Scoring

  • Matches consist of the best 3 games of 21 points
  • Every serve earns a point
  • A team that lands the shuttlecock on an opposing side, scores a point

Singles

Singles matches involve one person per team, two teams maximum.

  • Server serves from the right side of the court
  • If the server scores a point, they alternate sides of the court for serving

Doubles

Doubles matches involve two people per team, two teams maximum.

  • Each side only gets one serve per round
  • The first serve is done from the right side of the court. Subsequent serves are positioned corresponding to the score of the team serving; even = right, odd = left

History

The origins of the game of badminton date back over 2000 years with roots in Greece, China, and India. Rackets were often made out of sanded down tree branches and horse hair. Tournaments drew in travelers from all around the world and teams competed for a variety of winnings including: livestock, slaves, petty cash and tubs of Nutella.
